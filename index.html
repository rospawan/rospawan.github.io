Welcome

Hello, I'm Ali Rospawan

Ali Rospawan
PhD Researcher · Electrical Engineer · Lecturer · Lifelong Learner

I'm an electrical engineer and PhD researcher at National Chung Hsing University, Taiwan, working at the intersection of intelligent control systems, fuzzy neural networks, and autonomous mobile robots. My work spans industrial automation — extrusion barrels and semiconductor heating ovens — as well as visual SLAM and advanced localization methods for AMRs. With over six years of industry experience in PLC programming and automation design, I'm drawn to control problems that have real, deployable value.

Research Focus

What I Work On

⚙️

Intelligent Control & Fuzzy Neural Networks

Adaptive PID, fractional-order, and sliding-mode controllers powered by polynomial fuzzy and broad learning systems. Applied to real industrial machines — temperature control of extrusion barrels in plastic injection molding, semiconductor heating ovens, and DC motor positioning.

🤖

Autonomous Mobile Robots & Vision-Based Sensing

Trajectory following and cooperative localization for omnidirectional AMRs under uncertainty, using fuzzy TD3, GRU-attention, and fuzzy DDEKF approaches. Visual SLAM pipelines that convert RGB images into depth maps and 3D point clouds for autonomous robotic systems.
